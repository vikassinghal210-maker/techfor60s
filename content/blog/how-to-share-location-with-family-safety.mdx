## Why Share Your Location?

Location sharing isn't about surveillance — it's about **peace of mind**. Many seniors and their families use it so that:

- Family knows you arrived home safely after a trip
- Someone can find you quickly in an emergency
- You can see when a family member is on their way to visit
- If you ever get lost or disoriented, help can find you

You control who can see your location, and you can turn it off anytime.

## On iPhone: Using Find My

### Setting Up Location Sharing

1. Open the **Find My** app (it's pre-installed on every iPhone)
2. Tap the **People** tab at the bottom
3. Tap **Share My Location**
4. Type the name or phone number of the family member you want to share with
5. Choose **Share Indefinitely** (so you don't have to set it up again)
6. Tap **Send**

The other person will get a notification and can choose to share their location back with you.

### What the Other Person Sees

They can open their Find My app and see your location on a map. They can also get notifications when you arrive at or leave specific places (like home).

### How to Stop Sharing

1. Open **Find My** → **People** tab
2. Tap the person's name
3. Tap **Stop Sharing My Location**

## On Android: Using Google Maps

### Setting Up Location Sharing

1. Open **Google Maps**
2. Tap your **profile picture** (top right)
3. Tap **Location sharing**
4. Tap **Share location**
5. Choose **Until you turn this off**
6. Select the family member from your contacts (they need a Google account)
7. Tap **Share**

### What the Other Person Sees

They can open Google Maps, tap their profile picture → Location sharing, and see your location on the map.

### How to Stop Sharing

1. Open Google Maps → Profile → Location sharing
2. Tap the person you're sharing with
3. Tap **Stop**

## Family Setup: Both Sides

For location sharing to work best, **both people should share with each other**. This way:
- Your daughter can see that you're home safe
- You can see when she's on her way to visit
- It's a two-way street — not one-sided monitoring

## Setting Up Location-Based Notifications

### On iPhone (Find My)
1. In Find My → People → tap the person's name
2. Tap **Add** under Notifications
3. Choose to be notified when they **arrive at** or **leave** a location
4. Select the location (like their home)

This means you'll get a notification like "John has arrived home" automatically.

### On Android (Google Maps)
Location sharing in Google Maps shows real-time location but doesn't have automatic notifications. For that, use the **Google Family Link** app.

## Privacy Controls

You're always in control:

- **Turn it off anytime** — One tap to stop sharing
- **Choose who sees you** — Share with specific people only
- **No history** — Sharing shows current location, not where you've been
- **Battery impact** — Minimal; designed to use very little battery

## Tips for Families

1. **Discuss it openly** — Have a conversation about why location sharing helps everyone
2. **Make it mutual** — Both sides share, so it feels like teamwork, not monitoring
3. **Set expectations** — Agree on when to check (e.g., "I just want to know you got home safe")
4. **Respect privacy** — Don't comment on every movement or ask "Where were you?"
5. **Keep it simple** — Use the built-in tools (Find My or Google Maps) rather than third-party apps

## For Emergencies

If a family member's phone shows a location but they're not responding:

1. **Try calling** them first
2. **Send a text** — "Just checking on you, please reply when you can"
3. If no response and you're worried, **contact local police** for a welfare check
4. **Don't panic** — Phones sometimes show old locations if the battery died

Location sharing is one of the simplest safety tools available. It takes 2 minutes to set up and provides ongoing peace of mind for the whole family.
