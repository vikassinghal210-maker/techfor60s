T-Mobile is one of the most senior-friendly cell carriers in the United States. Their dedicated **55+ plan** is designed specifically for people 55 and older — and it's one of the cheapest unlimited plans on the market.

If you're 55 or older and live in Florida, or if you just want to compare every T-Mobile option available to seniors, this guide covers everything you need to know.

| Feature | Details |

## What the T-Mobile 55+ Plan Includes

### Unlimited Talk and Text
You get unlimited calls and texts to any number in the US. There are no per-minute charges or overage fees — ever.

### Unlimited Data (with a catch)
Your data is technically unlimited, but once you use **50GB in a month**, T-Mobile may slow your speeds during busy times. For most seniors who use email, Facebook, video calls, and streaming at home, you'll never hit that limit.

### 15GB Mobile Hotspot
You can share your phone's internet connection with a laptop or tablet. The first 15GB per month is at full speed.

### International Texting
Free texts to over 200 countries are included. Calls abroad cost extra.

### No Annual Contract
You can cancel any month with no penalty. This is standard across all T-Mobile plans.

## Best T-Mobile Plans for Non-Florida Seniors

If you're outside Florida and don't qualify for the 55+ plan, here are the best standard T-Mobile options for seniors:

### 1. T-Mobile Essentials (Cheapest Option)
- **Price:** ~$60/month for 1 line (drops with multiple lines)
- **Data:** Unlimited, but deprioritized at any time (not just after 50GB)
- **Hotspot:** 3G speeds only
- **Best for:** Seniors who mostly use WiFi and want basic unlimited service

### 2. T-Mobile Go5G (Mid-Range)
- **Price:** ~$75/month for 1 line
- **Data:** Unlimited, prioritized (deprioritized after 50GB)
- **Hotspot:** 15GB high-speed
- **Best for:** Seniors who travel and want reliable data speeds

### 3. T-Mobile Go5G Plus (Premium)
- **Price:** ~$90/month for 1 line
- **Data:** Unlimited premium (deprioritized after 100GB)
- **Hotspot:** 50GB high-speed
- **Travel:** Free unlimited data in 215+ countries
- **Best for:** Heavy data users or frequent travelers

## T-Mobile Home Internet: A Separate Option

T-Mobile also offers **home internet service** — this replaces your home broadband (like Comcast or Xfinity). It uses the cell network to bring WiFi to your home via a small box.

- **Price:** $50/month (locked in as long as you keep the service — no price hikes)
- **Speeds:** Typically 100–300 Mbps (enough for streaming, video calls, and browsing)

## How to Sign Up for T-Mobile as a Senior

### Option 1: In Store (Recommended for Seniors)
Walk into any T-Mobile store. Staff will help you:
1. Pick the right plan
2. Transfer your existing number (called "porting")
3. Set up your new phone or SIM card

**Bring with you:** A photo ID, your current phone (if keeping it), and your current carrier's account number.

### Option 2: Online
Visit t-mobile.com, choose your plan, and follow the checkout steps. If you already have an unlocked phone, you only need to order a SIM card ($0 with activation).

### Option 3: By Phone
Call T-Mobile customer service at **1-800-866-2453**. They can walk you through the process.

---

## Can You Keep Your Current Phone Number?

Yes. When you switch to T-Mobile, you can keep your existing phone number. This is called **number porting**. Just don't cancel your current plan before switching — T-Mobile handles the transfer for you.

## Does T-Mobile Offer Military or AARP Discounts?

T-Mobile offers an **AARP member discount**: AARP members get **10% off** certain T-Mobile plans. This applies to standard plans (not the 55+ plan, which already has its own pricing). If you're an AARP member outside Florida, this discount can meaningfully lower your bill.

**Military veterans** get additional discounts through T-Mobile's Military plan — ask in-store for details.
