## Internet Essentials: Comcast's Low-Income Program

Comcast launched Internet Essentials in 2011 as a commitment to affordable internet access. It has connected millions of households to the internet at a fixed low price.

### Who Qualifies?

Any household with at least one member participating in:

- ✅ Medicaid
- ✅ SNAP / EBT (food stamps)
- ✅ Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- ✅ Federal Public Housing Assistance / Section 8
- ✅ National School Lunch Program (NSLP)
- ✅ Veterans Pension or Survivors Pension
- ✅ Other income-qualified federal assistance programs

Many seniors on fixed incomes who receive SSI, SNAP, or Medicaid qualify automatically.

### What Internet Essentials Includes

- **100 Mbps download / 20 Mbps upload**
- **$9.95/month** (never increases while you remain eligible)
- **No data caps**
- **No credit check**
- **Free basic modem/router** (or option to upgrade to Xfinity Gateway for $14/month)
- **No contract** — cancel anytime

### Is 100 Mbps Fast Enough?

For most seniors, yes — absolutely. Here's what 100 Mbps handles easily:

| Activity | What You Need | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Email / web browsing | 1 Mbps | 100x more than needed |
| Netflix HD streaming | 5 Mbps | 20x more than needed |
| FaceTime / Zoom video call | 3 Mbps | 33x more than needed |
| Multiple devices at once | 25 Mbps | 4x more than needed |

100 Mbps is genuinely fast enough for a household of 1–4 people doing typical everyday internet activities.

## How to Apply for Internet Essentials {#how-to-apply}

### Online Application (Easiest Method)
1. Go to **xfinity.com/internet-essentials**
2. Click "Apply Now"
3. Enter your home address to confirm Comcast serves your area
4. Choose how to verify eligibility:
   - Enter government benefits information, OR
   - Upload a document (benefits card, award letter)
5. Choose your equipment option
6. Service activates within a few days

### By Phone
Call the Internet Essentials hotline: **1-855-846-8376** (available 8am–midnight ET, 7 days a week). A representative will walk you through the application.

### What Documents Do You Need?
- Medicaid card or benefits letter
- SNAP/EBT card or award letter
- SSI award letter
- Veterans benefits letter
- Or any document showing current enrollment in a qualifying program

## Tips to Save on Standard Comcast Plans

If you don't qualify for Internet Essentials, here's how to reduce a standard Comcast bill:

### Bring Your Own Modem
Comcast charges a **$15/month equipment rental fee** for their gateway/router. Buying a compatible modem (a one-time ~$70–100 cost) eliminates this forever. Over 2 years, that's $360 saved.

Popular compatible modems: Netgear CM600, Arris SB8200, Motorola MB7621.

### Call and Negotiate
When your promotional rate expires (usually after 12–24 months), your bill jumps. Before it does, call Comcast and say: "My promotion is ending and I'm considering switching providers. What deals are available?" Retention agents often extend promotional pricing or offer new deals.

### Check for Competing Providers
Even mentioning that you're comparing providers gives you more negotiating leverage. In areas with AT&T Fiber or another competitor, Comcast is more likely to offer better rates to keep your business.
