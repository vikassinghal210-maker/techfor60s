Your iPhone woke up this morning looking slightly different. Maybe the Control Center has new icons. Maybe the Photos app is rearranged. Maybe there is a new sparkle icon in Messages you did not ask for. That is an iOS update — a software refresh Apple pushes out a few times a year.

The good news: nothing is broken. Your contacts, messages, photos and apps are all still there.

This guide explains what is new in the current version of iOS in plain English, which features actually matter for seniors, and how to pause automatic updates if you prefer the stability of not having your phone change overnight.

Note: iOS versions are updated regularly. As of writing this guide (April 2026), the current public version is iOS 18.5. Some of what we describe may have been introduced in earlier iOS 18 updates. Check **Settings → General → About** to see your exact version.

## What actually changed in iOS 18 (the senior-relevant bits)

Apple ships dozens of features with each update. Most are for developers or for photographers. These are the handful that affect day-to-day senior use:

### 1. Bigger Control Center

Swipe down from the top-right of the screen and you get Control Center — the quick-settings panel with Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, flashlight, brightness. In iOS 18 this is now larger and customisable. You can:

- Drag icons around to put the flashlight where your thumb lands
- Add or remove tiles (take off the ones you never use)
- Long-press any tile to see more options

**Senior tip:** Put the flashlight, brightness slider and calculator near the top — those three get used most often.

### 2. Photos app reorganisation

The Photos app was redesigned in iOS 18. The old tabs (Library, For You, Albums, Search) are now a single scrolling page with sections below.

If this feels confusing:
- **Settings → Photos** (in the phone&apos;s Settings, not inside the app)
- There is no &quot;classic view&quot; toggle, unfortunately — but you can pin **People &amp; Pets**, **Recent Days** and **Albums** to the top so you can find what you need.

### 3. Apple Intelligence features (on supported iPhones)

Apple Intelligence is Apple&apos;s answer to ChatGPT, built into the phone. It requires an iPhone 15 Pro or newer. Features include:

- **Writing Tools** — rewrite a message to sound friendlier, proofread for typos
- **Smart Reply in Mail** — suggested one-tap responses
- **Siri upgrade** — Siri can now understand more natural questions
- **Image Playground** — create cartoon-style images

For most seniors, **Writing Tools** is the useful one. See [how to use Apple Intelligence on iPhone](/blog/how-to-use-apple-intelligence-iphone) for the walkthrough.

### 4. RCS messaging (finally)

iOS 18 added RCS support — meaning messages to Android users can now have read receipts, typing indicators, and high-quality photo sending. Previously, text to Android friends showed as green bubbles with poor photo quality.

**No setting to change.** The improvement happens automatically when both sides are on recent software.

### 5. Scheduled messages

You can now schedule a message to send later. Useful for birthdays and anniversaries.

- Type the message in Messages
- Tap and hold the send (blue arrow) button
- Choose &quot;Send Later&quot; and set the date / time

### 6. Passwords app (new)

Apple added a dedicated **Passwords** app. It replaces the old buried-in-settings password list. Now you have a proper home for all your saved logins.

Our [password manager setup guide](/blog/password-manager-setup-walkthrough-1password-bitwarden) compares this to Bitwarden and 1Password — the Apple Passwords app is fine for Apple-only households; if you use a Windows computer too, a third-party manager is better.

## What to do if the update broke something

### Your wallpaper looks different

Apple occasionally changes the default wallpaper. Reset via **Settings → Wallpaper** and choose your preferred one.

### Control Center is confusing

**Settings → Control Center → Customise Controls** — drag icons into the order you want. You can also delete tiles you never use (Home app, Low Power Mode toggle, etc.).

### You cannot find the app you always used

The update sometimes hides older apps in the App Library. Swipe left to the very end of your home screen — the App Library shows every app grouped by category. Drag the one you want back onto the home screen.

### Battery drain seems worse

New iOS versions often cause 2–3 days of worse battery while the phone re-indexes photos and re-downloads apps in the background. Give it 72 hours before worrying. If still bad after a week: **Settings → Battery → Battery Health** and check that maximum capacity is above 80%.

## How to pause automatic updates

If you prefer to wait a month before each update — so Apple can fix any initial bugs before the update reaches you — turn off auto-update:

1. **Settings → General → Software Update**
2. Tap **Automatic Updates**
3. Turn **OFF** both:
   - Download iOS updates
   - Install iOS updates

You will then see a notification when updates are available, and you can install them when you are ready.

**Note:** Keep **Security Responses &amp; System Files** turned ON. Those are urgent security patches that you want installed automatically.

## How to check your iOS version

**Settings → General → About → iOS Version**

If you are not on the current version, and your iPhone is one of the supported models (iPhone XS or newer as of 2026), you can update via **Settings → General → Software Update**.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Do I have to install every iOS update?

You do not have to install feature updates immediately. But you should install security updates when they become available — these fix vulnerabilities scammers can exploit. If you turn off auto-update, check **Settings → General → Software Update** once a month to see what is pending.

### Can I go back to the previous iOS version if I dislike the update?

Generally no. Apple closes the ability to downgrade within a few weeks of a new version&apos;s release. This is why we suggest waiting a month before installing a major new iOS — it lets other users find and report bugs first.

### My phone updated and now the Photos app looks strange. How do I fix it?

The Photos app was redesigned in iOS 18. Unfortunately there is no &quot;classic view&quot; option. The best workaround: inside Photos, scroll down to **Pinned Collections** and customise which sections appear at the top — pin the ones you use (Recent Days, People, Albums) and the app becomes much more usable.

### What is Apple Intelligence and do I have it?

Apple Intelligence is Apple&apos;s built-in AI, added in iOS 18.1. It requires an iPhone 15 Pro or newer, or an iPad with an M1 chip or later. If you have an older iPhone, Apple Intelligence does not appear on your phone — and most of what it does can be replicated by using ChatGPT or Claude apps separately.

### Will my iPhone still get updates if it is a few years old?

Apple typically supports iPhones for 5–6 years of iOS updates. As of iOS 18 (2026), all iPhones from iPhone XS (2018) onwards still receive the full iOS 18 update. Older phones are dropped in later versions.

### How do I know if my iPhone model is compatible with the latest iOS?

Go to **Settings → General → Software Update**. If your iPhone is compatible with the latest iOS, you will see the update option there. If not, you will see a message stating that your phone is running the latest version available for your model.
