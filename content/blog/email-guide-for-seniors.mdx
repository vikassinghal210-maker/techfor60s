## Why Email Still Matters

Even with text messaging and social media, email remains essential:

- **Official communications** — Banks, doctors, insurance, and government agencies use email
- **Account verification** — Every online account needs an email address
- **Sending documents** — Email handles photos, PDFs, and documents better than texting
- **Permanent record** — Emails stay in your inbox as a searchable record
- **No phone number needed** — Email anyone in the world with just their email address
- **Free** — All major email services are completely free

## Choosing an Email Service

### Gmail (Recommended)

- **Best for:** Everyone, especially Android phone users
- **Free storage:** 15GB (enough for years of email)
- **Works with:** Google Calendar, Google Photos, Google Drive
- **Website:** gmail.com

### Yahoo Mail

- **Best for:** People who already have a Yahoo account
- **Free storage:** 1TB (very generous)
- **Website:** mail.yahoo.com

### Outlook (Microsoft)

- **Best for:** People who use Microsoft products
- **Free storage:** 15GB
- **Works with:** Microsoft 365, Calendar, OneDrive
- **Website:** outlook.com

> **Our recommendation:** If you're starting fresh, choose **Gmail**. It's the most popular, most reliable, and works seamlessly with Android phones and Google services.

## Creating a Gmail Account

1. Go to **gmail.com**
2. Click **"Create account"**
3. Enter your first and last name
4. Choose a username — this becomes your email address (like janedoe@gmail.com)
5. Create a password (see our [password guide](/blog/how-to-create-strong-passwords) for tips)
6. Add a phone number (for account recovery — highly recommended)
7. Add a recovery email (if you have another email)
8. Enter your birthday and gender
9. Accept the terms
10. You're done!

> **Username tip:** Try your name with a number: janedoe1960@gmail.com or jane.doe.smith@gmail.com. Simple names are often taken, so adding numbers or your middle name helps.

## Sending an Email

### On Your Computer (Gmail)

1. Go to **gmail.com** and sign in
2. Click the **"Compose"** button (top left)
3. In **"To:"** — type the recipient's email address
4. In **"Subject:"** — type a brief description (like "Photos from Sunday" or "Question about appointment")
5. In the large text area — type your message
6. Click **"Send"** (blue button)

### On Your Phone

1. Open the **Gmail** app
2. Tap the **"Compose"** button (pencil icon, usually bottom right)
3. Fill in To, Subject, and your message
4. Tap the **send arrow**

## Attaching Files and Photos

### On Computer

1. Compose a new email
2. Click the **paperclip icon** at the bottom of the compose window
3. Navigate to the file or photo on your computer
4. Select it and click **"Open"**
5. The file appears attached to your email
6. Click **"Send"**

### On Phone

1. Compose a new email
2. Tap the **paperclip icon** (top of the compose window)
3. Choose **"Attach file"** or **"Insert from Photos"**
4. Select the photo or file
5. Send

> **File size limit:** Gmail allows attachments up to 25MB. For larger files, Gmail automatically creates a Google Drive link instead.

## Reading and Replying to Emails

### Reading
1. Open Gmail
2. Tap or click any email in your inbox to read it
3. The email opens in full

### Replying
1. Open the email you want to reply to
2. Click **"Reply"** (or the curved arrow icon)
3. Type your response
4. Click **"Send"**

### Reply vs. Reply All
- **Reply** — Goes only to the person who sent the email
- **Reply All** — Goes to everyone who received the email
- **Be careful with Reply All** — Only use it when everyone needs to see your response

### Forwarding
To send an email you received to someone else:
1. Open the email
2. Click **"Forward"**
3. Enter the new recipient's email
4. Add a note if needed ("Thought you might find this interesting")
5. Send

## Organizing Your Inbox

### Creating Labels (Folders)

1. In Gmail, look at the left sidebar
2. Scroll down and click **"+ Create new label"**
3. Name it (e.g., "Medical," "Family," "Bills," "Recipes")
4. To label an email: open it → click the **label icon** (tag) → select the label

### Starring Important Emails

1. Click the **star** next to any email
2. Find all starred emails by clicking **"Starred"** in the left sidebar
3. Great for marking emails you need to come back to

### Archiving Old Emails

If your inbox feels cluttered but you don't want to delete emails:
1. Select the email
2. Click the **archive button** (box with down arrow)
3. The email is removed from your inbox but still searchable
4. Find archived emails by searching or clicking **"All Mail"**

### Searching for Emails

1. Click the search bar at the top of Gmail
2. Type keywords: a person's name, a subject, or specific words
3. Press Enter
4. Gmail searches all your emails and shows matches

**Advanced search tips:**
- `from:john` — Emails from John
- `has:attachment` — Emails with attachments
- `before:2025/01/01` — Emails before a date
- `subject:appointment` — Emails with "appointment" in the subject

## Avoiding Email Scams

### Red Flags

1. **"Urgent! Your account will be closed!"** — Scammers create urgency to make you act without thinking
2. **Requests for personal information** — Your bank will NEVER ask for your password or Social Security number by email
3. **Strange sender addresses** — "amazon-support@gmail.com" is NOT from Amazon. Real Amazon emails come from @amazon.com
4. **Spelling mistakes and poor grammar** — Legitimate companies proofread their emails
5. **Suspicious links** — Hover over links (don't click!) to see where they really go. If the URL looks strange, don't click.
6. **"You won a prize!"** — You didn't enter a contest, you didn't win anything
7. **Threatening language** — "Pay now or face legal action" from an unknown sender

### Safe Email Practices

- **Never click links** in emails from unknown senders
- **Never download attachments** from unknown senders
- **Never share passwords** via email — no legitimate company asks for this
- **When in doubt, go directly** — If an email claims to be from your bank, don't click the link. Instead, type your bank's website directly in your browser.
- **Mark spam as spam** — Click "Report spam" to train your email filter

## Managing Subscriptions

### Unsubscribing from Unwanted Emails

1. Open the unwanted email
2. Scroll to the very bottom
3. Look for a small **"Unsubscribe"** link
4. Click it and follow the instructions
5. Gmail also shows an **"Unsubscribe"** link at the top of some marketing emails

### Blocking Senders

If someone keeps emailing you:
1. Open their email
2. Click the **three dots** (⋮) next to the reply button
3. Click **"Block [sender name]"**
4. Their emails go straight to spam

## Email Settings for Seniors

### Increase Display Density

1. Click the **gear icon** (top right) → **"See all settings"**
2. Under **"Display density"** choose **"Comfortable"** for more spacing

### Set a Signature

A signature is text that appears at the bottom of every email you send:

1. Settings → General → Scroll down to **"Signature"**
2. Click **"Create new"**
3. Type your name, phone number, or any info you want included
4. Example:
   ```
   Best regards,
   Jane Doe
   (555) 123-4567
   ```
5. Save changes

### Enable Undo Send

Gmail lets you unsend an email for a few seconds:
1. Settings → General → **"Undo Send"**
2. Set the cancellation period to **30 seconds**
3. Now after sending, you'll see an "Undo" option for 30 seconds

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I change my email password?

Go to myaccount.google.com → Security → "How you sign in to Google" → Password. Enter your current password, then create a new one. Do this if you think someone else has access to your account.

### Can I have more than one email address?

Yes! Many people have a personal email and a separate one for online shopping, newsletters, or accounts. You can create multiple Gmail accounts for free.

### What happens if I delete an email?

Deleted emails go to the **Trash** folder and stay there for 30 days. After 30 days, they're permanently deleted. If you accidentally delete something, check Trash immediately.

### How much storage do I get?

Gmail gives 15GB free, shared across Gmail, Google Drive, and Google Photos. That's enough for most people for many years. If you run out, you can buy more (100GB for $1.99/month) or delete old emails with large attachments.

### Can someone hack my email?

They can try, but you can make it very difficult. Use a strong, unique password, enable two-factor authentication (Settings → Security → 2-Step Verification), and never click suspicious links. With these precautions, your email is very secure.
