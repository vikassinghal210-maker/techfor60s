## How to Apply the Verizon AARP Discount

The discount **does not apply automatically** when you sign up with Verizon — you have to register it separately.

### Step 1: Join or Confirm AARP Membership
You need an active AARP membership. AARP membership costs $12/year for the first year. Sign up or log in at aarp.org.

### Step 2: Register Your Discount
1. Go to **verizon.com/discounts** (or search "Verizon AARP discount")
2. Sign in with your My Verizon account
3. Enter your AARP membership number
4. Click "Apply Discount"

Verizon verifies your AARP membership. The discount typically shows up on your next bill.

### Step 3: Verify on Your Bill
Check your next Verizon bill to confirm the discount is applied. Look for "AARP Member Benefit" in the monthly charges.

If you have trouble, call Verizon at **1-800-922-0204** and tell them you're an AARP member who wants to apply the discount.

## Which Verizon Plans Does the AARP Discount Apply To?

The AARP discount applies to Verizon's main postpaid unlimited plans:
- ✅ Welcome Unlimited
- ✅ Unlimited Plus
- ✅ Unlimited Ultimate
- ✅ myPlan (newer flexible plan structure)

It generally does NOT apply to:
- ❌ Prepaid plans
- ❌ Verizon Fios home internet (separate service)
- ❌ Device payment installments

When in doubt, ask a Verizon representative to confirm which plans the discount applies to before signing up.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do Verizon and AARP have a discount deal?
Yes. AARP members receive 10% off monthly line access fees on qualifying Verizon postpaid plans. You must register the discount yourself — it isn't automatic.

### How do I register my AARP discount with Verizon?
Sign in at verizon.com/discounts, enter your AARP membership number, and apply. Or call Verizon at 1-800-922-0204 and a representative will apply it for you.

### Does the Verizon AARP discount apply to prepaid plans?
No. The AARP discount applies to postpaid (monthly billed) plans only, not Verizon prepaid plans.

### Is the Verizon AARP discount worth it?
The 10% saves $78–$108/year on a single line — meaningful savings, but Verizon remains more expensive than competitors. Whether it's "worth it" depends on whether Verizon's superior rural coverage matters to you.

### Can I combine the AARP discount with other Verizon promotions?
Sometimes. Promotional device deals (trade-ins, new line offers) are usually separate from the AARP membership discount. Ask a Verizon representative about stacking discounts.
