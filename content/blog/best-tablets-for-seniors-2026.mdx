A tablet is often easier to use than a laptop and more comfortable to read than a phone. The larger screen makes everything clearer — from reading the news to video calling your grandchildren.

We tested 8 tablets and narrowed it down to the 5 best options for adults over 60. Here is what we found.

## What to Look For in a Tablet

Before we get to our picks, here is what matters most for seniors:

- **Screen size**: 10 inches or larger. Bigger screens are easier to read and navigate.
- **Weight**: Under 500g (about 1 pound). You will hold it a lot — lighter is better.
- **Battery life**: 8+ hours. You should not need to charge it every day.
- **Simple interface**: Easy to find apps, large icons, clear text.
- **Price**: You do not need to spend a fortune. Great tablets start at around $150.

## Our Top 5 Picks

### 1. Apple iPad 10th Generation — Best Overall

The standard iPad is the best all-around tablet for most seniors. The 10.9-inch screen is bright and sharp, the interface is intuitive, and it works beautifully for video calls, reading, and watching videos.

**Why we like it:**
- Very smooth and responsive
- Huge app selection
- FaceTime video calling built in
- 10-hour battery life
- Apple stores offer free in-person help

**Price:** Around $349

### 2. Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+ — Best Value

If you prefer Android or want to save money, the Galaxy Tab A9+ offers a large 11-inch screen at roughly half the price of an iPad.

**Why we like it:**
- Large 11-inch screen
- Affordable price point
- Familiar if you already use a Samsung phone
- Expandable storage with a memory card

**Price:** Around $179

### 3. Amazon Fire HD 10 — Best Budget Option

The Fire HD 10 costs under $100 and does everything most seniors need: web browsing, email, video calls, reading, and streaming TV shows.

**Why we like it:**
- Very affordable
- Alexa voice assistant built in — just ask it questions
- Good for reading books (Kindle app)
- Parental controls if grandchildren use it too

**Price:** Around $89

### 4. Apple iPad Air — Best Premium Choice

If budget is not a concern, the iPad Air has a stunning screen, faster performance, and works with the Apple Pencil if you want to write or draw.

**Why we like it:**
- Beautiful 11-inch display
- Very fast — will last years without slowing down
- Thin and lightweight
- Face ID unlock — no need to type a password

**Price:** Around $599

### 5. GrandPad — Designed Specifically for Seniors

GrandPad is a tablet built from the ground up for seniors. It comes with a simplified interface, a built-in cellular connection (no WiFi needed), and a dedicated support phone line.

**Why we like it:**
- Extremely simple interface with large buttons
- Comes with cellular data — no WiFi setup needed
- Family members can manage it remotely
- 24/7 phone support included

**Price:** Around $74/month (includes the tablet and data plan)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need WiFi to use a tablet?**
Most tablets need WiFi for internet. The exception is GrandPad, which has its own cellular connection. You can also buy "cellular" versions of iPads and Samsung tablets for an extra cost.

**Can I make phone calls on a tablet?**
You can make video and voice calls using apps like WhatsApp, Zoom, or FaceTime. You cannot make regular phone calls unless your tablet has a cellular connection.

**How long do tablets last?**
A good tablet should last 4-6 years before it starts feeling slow. iPads tend to receive software updates the longest (5-6 years).
