## What Is a Ring Doorbell?

A Ring doorbell is a video doorbell that replaces or attaches next to your existing doorbell. When someone comes to your door, it sends a notification to your phone so you can see and talk to them from anywhere — whether you are in the kitchen, in the backyard, or away from home.

For seniors, a Ring doorbell provides safety and convenience. You can see who is at the door without getting up, talk to delivery drivers without opening the door, and feel more secure knowing your front entrance is monitored.

## Which Ring Doorbell Should You Buy?

Ring makes several models. Here are the best options:

- **Ring Battery Doorbell ($99.99)** — Battery-powered, no wiring needed. Easiest to install. **Our top pick for most seniors.**
- **Ring Video Doorbell Wired ($64.99)** — Must be wired to existing doorbell wires. Smaller and more affordable, but requires basic wiring.
- **Ring Video Doorbell 4 ($199.99)** — Premium battery model with better video quality and Pre-Roll feature that shows a few seconds before the motion event.
- **Ring Video Doorbell Pro 2 ($249.99)** — Wired model with the best video quality, 3D motion detection, and bird's-eye view.

**Our recommendation:** The **Ring Battery Doorbell ($99.99)** is the best choice for most seniors because it requires no wiring — you simply mount it and connect to WiFi.

## What You Need

- A smartphone or tablet (iPhone or Android)
- WiFi internet at home
- A screwdriver (included with Ring)
- About 20 to 30 minutes

## Step 1: Download the Ring App

Before you touch the hardware, set up the app:

1. On your phone, go to the **App Store** (iPhone) or **Google Play Store** (Android).
2. Search for **"Ring"** and download the Ring app.
3. Open the app and create a **Ring account** with your email and a password.
4. Tap **"Set Up a Device"** and select **"Doorbells"**.
5. Select your Ring model from the list.

The app will walk you through the entire setup process with clear instructions and pictures.

## Step 2: Charge the Battery (Battery Models Only)

If you have a battery-powered Ring doorbell:

1. The battery comes partially charged. Plug the included USB cable into the doorbell (or remove the battery pack) and connect to a USB charger.
2. Charge until the light turns solid green — this takes about 5 to 10 hours for the first charge.
3. A full charge typically lasts 1 to 3 months depending on activity.

**Tip:** Consider buying a second battery ($29.99) so you always have one charged and ready to swap.

## Step 3: Install the Doorbell

### Battery-Powered Installation (No Wiring)

1. Use the included **level tool** and **mounting bracket** to mark where you want to place the Ring doorbell. Ideal height is about 4 feet from the ground.
2. Drill holes using the included drill bit (you can use the included screws directly for wood surfaces without drilling).
3. Screw the mounting bracket to the wall.
4. Snap the Ring doorbell onto the bracket until it clicks.
5. Secure it with the included **security screw** on the bottom using the included star-shaped screwdriver.

### Wired Installation

If you have existing doorbell wiring and chose a wired model:

1. Turn off power to your doorbell at the circuit breaker.
2. Remove your old doorbell.
3. Connect the two existing doorbell wires to the screws on the back of the Ring (it does not matter which wire goes to which screw).
4. Mount the Ring to the wall using the included bracket.
5. Turn the power back on.

**Tip for wired installation:** If you are not comfortable with wiring, ask a family member or hire a handyman. Ring also offers professional installation through their website.

## Step 4: Connect to WiFi

1. In the Ring app, follow the setup prompts. The app will tell you to press the **orange button** on the back of the Ring doorbell.
2. Your phone will temporarily connect to the Ring's own WiFi network.
3. Select your **home WiFi network** from the list in the app.
4. Enter your **WiFi password**.
5. Wait for the Ring to connect. The light on the front will spin blue, then turn solid.

**Important:** Ring doorbells work best when your WiFi router is within 20 to 30 feet. If your router is far from the front door, consider a WiFi extender or the Ring Chime Pro ($49.99), which extends your WiFi to your doorbell.

## Step 5: Customize Your Settings

In the Ring app, go to your doorbell's settings to adjust:

### Motion Settings
- **Motion sensitivity** — Set how sensitive the motion detection is. Start with the default and adjust if you get too many or too few alerts.
- **Motion zones** — Draw zones on a map of your front area to tell Ring which areas to watch. This prevents alerts from cars passing on the street.
- **Motion schedule** — Turn off motion alerts during certain times if needed.

### Notification Settings
- Choose whether you want alerts on your phone when motion is detected, when someone presses the doorbell, or both.
- You can set different notification sounds for motion vs. doorbell rings.

### Video Settings
- **Live View** — Open the app anytime to see a live view of your front door.
- **Video recording** — Requires a Ring Protect subscription (see below) to save and review recorded videos.

## Answering Your Door from Anywhere

When someone presses the doorbell or triggers a motion alert:

1. Your phone will show a notification.
2. Tap the notification to see **live video** of who is at the door.
3. Tap the **microphone icon** to talk to them through the doorbell speaker.
4. You can say things like "Please leave the package on the porch" or "I'll be right there."

The person at the door does not know if you are home or away — the audio sounds the same either way. This is a great security feature.

## Ring Protect Plans

Ring doorbells work without a subscription for live viewing and real-time alerts. However, if you want to save and review video recordings, you need a Ring Protect plan:

- **Ring Protect Basic ($3.99/month)** — Saves video from one doorbell for 180 days. Includes person detection.
- **Ring Protect Plus ($10/month)** — Saves video from all Ring devices at your home. Includes 24/7 professional monitoring and extended warranty.

**Our recommendation:** Start without a plan to see if you like it. Add the Basic plan ($3.99/month) if you want to review past recordings.

## Sharing Access with Family

You can give family members access to your Ring doorbell so they can also see who is at your door:

1. Open the Ring app and go to **Devices** > your doorbell.
2. Tap **Device Settings** > **Shared Users**.
3. Tap **"Add User"** and enter their email address.
4. They will receive an invitation to create a Ring account and access your doorbell.

Shared users can view live video, receive alerts, and talk to visitors — but they cannot change your device settings unless you give them "Owner" access.

## Indoor Chime Setup

If you cannot hear your phone notifications, consider adding a **Ring Chime ($29.99)** — a small plug-in device that plays a sound inside your home whenever someone presses the doorbell or triggers motion. Simply plug it into any wall outlet and connect it through the Ring app.

## Tips for Seniors

- **Use Quick Replies** — Set up pre-recorded messages like "Please leave the package at the door" that you can play with one tap instead of speaking live.
- **Enable Rich Notifications** — On iPhone, these show a snapshot from the camera right in the notification, so you can see who it is without opening the app.
- **Check battery monthly** — The Ring app shows battery level. When it drops below 20%, bring it inside to charge.
- **Clean the camera** — Wipe the lens with a soft cloth every few weeks to keep the picture clear.
- **Nighttime visibility** — Ring doorbells have infrared night vision, so you can see visitors even in the dark.

## Troubleshooting

**Not getting notifications:** Check that notifications are enabled in both the Ring app and your phone's settings. Make sure Do Not Disturb is off.

**Video is blurry or laggy:** Your WiFi signal may be weak at the door. Move your router closer or add a WiFi extender.

**Battery drains quickly:** Reduce motion sensitivity or create smaller motion zones so the camera activates less often.

**Doorbell not ringing inside:** If you have a wired Ring and your indoor chime does not ring, check the Chime Type setting in the Ring app.

## The Bottom Line

A Ring doorbell lets you see and talk to anyone at your front door without getting up or opening the door — a real safety upgrade for seniors living alone or anyone who wants more peace of mind. The battery-powered model takes about 20 minutes to install with no wiring needed, and the Ring app makes it simple to see and respond to visitors from anywhere. It is one of the most practical smart home upgrades you can make.
